The Rainbow Flag

Flag History and Meaning

The original rainbow flag was created by Gilbert Baker in 1978. Baker created the flag for the Gay Freedom Day celebration in San Francisco, after receiving appeals from City Supervisor Harvey Milk and other local activists. The flag was hand-stitched and dyed with the help of Lynn Segerblom (Faerie Argyle Rainbow), James McNamara, Glenne McElhinney, Joe Duran, Paul Langlotz, and other friends and volunteers. It was first raised on June 25, 1978 in the United Nations Plaza in San Francisco.

The each color of the Gilbert Baker pride flag has its own meaning:

  • Pink: sex
  • Red: life
  • Orange: healing
  • Yellow: sunlight
  • Green: nature
  • Turquoise: art, magic
  • Blue/indigo: serenity
  • Purple: spirit
