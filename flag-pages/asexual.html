Asexual

Definiton

Disclaimer: People can experience identities in different ways. As such, people can identify with a label without exactly matching the definition because identities are fluid.

Asexual (often shortened to "ace") is a term used by people who experience little, no, and/or conditional sexual attraction. Asexual people may still experience other forms of attraction; the term simply refers to a person's sexual orientation. Asexual people may engage in sexual activities and may experience arousal/libido. Some asexual people enjoy sexual activities (they are sex-favorable), some feel negatively about them (sex-repulsed and/or sex-averse), some feel neutral about them (sex-indifferent), and some have mixed feelings about them (sex-ambivalent).

People who are not asexual are called allosexual.

Flag History and Meaning

The asexual pride flag was chosen in August 2010 on The Asexual Visibility & Education Network (AVEN) forum. AVEN users standup and Bristrek led the effort to choose an asexual flag, which lasted a month, spanned multiple threads, and included 3 poll stages. The winning flag was created by standup. The colors were originally based upon the AVEN Triangle, a popular symbol of asexuality at the time (which is black, gray, and white) and the purple accents of the AVEN website.

The each color of the asexual flag has its own meaning:

  • Black: asexuals
  • Gray: graysexuals and demisexuals
  • White: non-asexual partners and allies
  • Purple: the community
Related Identities

Asexual is an umbrella term for the asexual spectrum (ace spec) and includes labels such as:

  • Demisexual
  • Graysexual

Being asexual is also part of the aromantic-asexual (aroace) identity.
